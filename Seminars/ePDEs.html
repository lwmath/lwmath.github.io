Elliptic PDEs seminars

This is a student seminar organized by Ling Wang. In this semester, we mainly focus on the classical Minkowski problem. The reference of our seminar is the well-known book, The Minkowski Multidimensional Problem, written by Pogorelov.

The classical Minkowski problem is that of finding a closed convex hypersurface whose Gaussian curvature is a given positive function of the exterior unit normal with certain conditions. The generalized solution of this proble was obtained by Minkowski, Alexandrov and others. However, this last solution does not provide any information about the regularity of the (unique) convex hypersurface even if we assume Gaussian curvature function is analytic. Hence, a natural question arises as to whether there exists a regular hypersurface with regular Gaussian curvature.

This problem in the case of two-dimensional surfaces in three-dimensional space was studied by many well-known mathematicians: H. Lewy, C. Miranda, A. V. Pogorelov, L. Nirenberg, and others. In a series of papers, published by Pogorelov, a solution of the general (multidimensional) Minkowski problem has been given. The book we will read contains a complete exposition of the results announced in those papers.

The plan of our seminar is as follows. We will start with the basic definitions and results for convex bodies and hypersurfaces in the first lecture. In the second lecture, we reproduce the classic result of Minkowski. The third lecture will be related to regularities, and it is showed there that if the Gaussian curvature function is positive and sufficiently regular, then the solution of Minkowski's problem is also regular. Some generalization will be discussed in the forth lecture. In the fifth lecture, we will learn the famous Pogorelov type estimates for Monge-Ampere equations. The final lecture is devoted to the classical Liouville theorem of Monge-Ampere equations. It is proved that all convex solutions to the Monge-Ampere equation with constant right hand side in the whole space must be quadratic functions.
