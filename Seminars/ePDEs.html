Elliptic PDE seminars

This is a student seminar organized by Ling Wang. In this semester, we mainly focus on the classical Minkowski problem. The reference of our seminar is the well-known book, The Minkowski Multidimensional Problem, written by Pogorelov.

The classical Minkowski problem is that of finding a closed convex hypersurface whose Gaussian curvature is a given positive function of the exterior unit normal with certain conditions. The generalized solution of this proble was obtained by Minkowski, Alexandrov and others. However, this last solution does not provide any information about the regularity of the (unique) convex hypersurface even if we assume Gaussian curvature function is analytic. Hence, a natural question arises as to whether there exists a regular hypersurface with regular Gaussian curvature. This problem in the case of two-dimensional surfaces in three-dimensional space was studied by many well-known mathematicians: H. Lewy, C. Miranda, A. V. Pogorelov, L. Nirenberg, and others. In a series of papers, published by Pogorelov, a solution of the general (multidimensional) Minkowski problem has been given. The book we will read contains a complete exposition of the results announced in those papers.

10 Mar 2023, 9am Beijing time. Connor Mooney
(University of California at Irvine)		 The anisotropic Bernstein problem

Abstract: The Bernstein problem asks whether entire minimal graphs in R^{n+1} are necessarily hyperplanes. It is known through spectacular work of Bernstein, Fleming, De Giorgi, Almgren, Simons, and Bombieri-De Giorgi-Giusti that the answer is positive if and only if n < 8. The anisotropic Bernstein problem asks the same question about minimizers of parametric elliptic functionals, which are natural generalizations of the area functional that both arise in many applications and offer important technical challenges. We will discuss the recent solution of this problem (the answer is positive if and only if n < 4). This is joint work with Y. Yang.
